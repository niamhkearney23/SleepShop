An end-to-end hiring pipeline — applications in, offers out, zero admin drag.
Internal briefing · April 2026
The problem today

Recruitment eats billable hours.

  • Every CV is read manually, often twice.
  • No structured way to compare candidates on judgement or attitude.
  • Shortlisting, scheduling, and reply emails all live in separate inboxes.
  • Candidates wait days — sometimes weeks — for a decision.
  • Diviya and Niamh spend hours per week on admin that a system could handle.

Candidate experience

What they see.

  • One public link — shared on the website, LinkedIn, or direct.
  • 8 structured questions — about themselves, judgement scenarios, written exercises.
  • CV upload — stored securely in the firm's Drive.
  • Instant confirmation — "We've received your application."
  • 24 hours later — a personalised decision email arrives.

Three outcomes

Yes Invite to 15-min screening call via Calendly.

Borderline Senior review, decision within 7 days.

No Polite decline, encouragement to reapply.

Inside the assessment

Ten quality scores. One recommendation.

Critical Thinking

Logic, structure, and reasoning in written answers.

Written Communication

Clarity, tone, and professionalism.

Judgement

Handling of ambiguous, high-pressure scenarios.

Self-Awareness

Honest reflection on strengths and gaps.

Attitude & Motivation

Why law, why this firm, what drives them.

Innovation

Creative problem-solving, resourcefulness.

Attention to Detail

Spelling, grammar, factual precision.

Time Management

How they handle competing deadlines.

Resilience

Response to setbacks and feedback.

Plus Professional Integrity — scored separately. Every candidate receives a total out of 100 with a written rationale.

The safety net

Nothing goes out for 24 hours.

  • Every submission lands in the Applications sheet immediately.
  • The candidate email is marked SCHEDULED — not sent.
  • Niamh has a full day to override the AI's call.
  • To cancel: change one cell. That's it.
  • To send early: update the timestamp and run the queue.

Why this matters

AI gets most decisions right. The 24-hour window means partners remain in control of every single rejection and every single offer. No embarrassing auto-emails. No missed unicorns.

Behind the scenes

One sheet. Everything connected.

  • Applications sheet — every submission, colour-coded by decision (green / amber / red).
  • Candidate report — formatted Google Doc auto-generated per submission with all answers, scores, and AI rationale.
  • Resume — uploaded PDF saved to the firm's Drive folder, renamed with the candidate's name, linked from the sheet.
  • Calendly — candidates self-book screening calls; Google Meet or Zoom link auto-generated.
  • Audit trail — every status change, decision, and email is timestamped.

Risks & mitigations

What could go wrong — and why it won't.

AI misjudges a candidate

24-hour human review; every decision can be overridden in one click.

Candidate data privacy

All data stays inside the firm's own Google Workspace. No third-party database.

System downtime

Form submissions retry automatically; sheet entries are atomic. Zero data loss seen in testing.

Partners don't trust it

Pilot one recruitment cycle. Every row, score, and email is visible. Full transparency.
